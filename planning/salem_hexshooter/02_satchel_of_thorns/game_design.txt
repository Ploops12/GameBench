SATCHEL OF THORNS

Variant pitch
This version treats the witch as a feral hedge alchemist. Camp experimentation is messy, physical, and biological. Spells are assembled from living or semi-living ingredients and feel like cruel little organisms unleashed at enemies.

Combat fantasy
The player feels like a roadside hag weaponizing nests, thorns, larvae, fumes, and curse-pods. Combat is more improvised than the grimoire variant, and the spells often feel like they are hatching, latching, or spreading rather than simply firing.

Rest-time brewing focus
The camp phase revolves around physically staging ingredient reactions around the cauldron:
- a worktable for laying carriers and organs around a brew circle
- hanging satchels, stitched pockets, and specimen trays
- reaction bowls for watching how instincts and mutations alter a spell
- a stable recipe rack for preserved concoctions
- a hazard display showing if a mixture is likely to burst, recur, infest, or turn on the caster

Spell-building mechanism: Reagent Anatomy
Every spell is built from four biological roles:
- Carrier: thorn pod, moth cloud, bile flask, bone needle cluster, briar seed, skull mote
- Core Reagent: plague honey, bog marrow, saint ash, black milk, widow nettle, execution smoke
- Instinct: hunt, cling, burst, nest, leech, scatter, stalk
- Mutation: molts, sprouts, screams, divides, remembers, ferments

Emergent behavior comes from bodily logic:
- a moth cloud with hunt + screams becomes a panic swarm that spirals toward motion
- a thorn pod with cling + sprouts becomes a snaring bloom that grows off targets and walls
- a bile flask with burst + ferments creates delayed toxic eruptions after impact
- a briar seed with remembers can regrow at the place a victim died

The discovery loop is about observing behavior, not just reading tooltips. The player should feel like they are breeding harmful little miracles.

Combat brewing model: coat rummage
This variant never uses queued recipe pages. Combat brewing happens through a real-time satchel and coat interface:
- hold brew to open fast-access pockets and loops on the coat interior
- movement slows but continues
- sprinting and some vertical mobility are disabled
- carried ingredients are chosen by muscle memory from stitched physical slots
- the result is assembled quickly and thrown or cast

This creates a highly tactile, stressful improvisation layer.

Example spells
- Thorn Litter: seed burst that carpets the floor with ankle-binding growth
- Mourning Moths: shrieking swarm that induces panic and blocks sight
- Leech Flask: splatter curse that feeds on hurt enemies and returns ward
- Widow Husk: clinging pod that bursts into nettles when its host dies
- Rotten Memory: recurring bile mark that reopens where blood was spilled

Strengths
- Very witchy and weird
- Strong visual identity for both camp and combat
- Makes reagent collection feel exciting because each part changes behavior

Risks
- Must keep the coat interface readable under pressure
- Emergent creature-like logic needs concise feedback in camp testing
