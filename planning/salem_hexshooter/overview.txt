SALEM HEXSHOOTER

Core pitch
You are a witch fighting your way out of Salem in a fast, brutal boomer-shooter. Each run pushes through a chain of cursed districts, torchlit roads, farms, church yards, and fortified streets full of witch-hunters. Angry villagers become trained militias, sanctified executioners, alchemical suppressors, and anti-witch constructs built specifically to kill your kind.

This is not a gun game with spell skins. The player never finds weapons. The whole combat kit comes from hexes, curses, omens, blights, familiars, and ritual spellforms built from harvested reagents.

Run structure
1. Enter a Salem district and clear an aggressive combat space.
2. Collect reagents from bodies, shrines, cursed flora, reliquaries, and witch-hunter gear.
3. Return to the witch camp, built around a creaking carriage and a central cauldron.
4. Experiment with reagents, discover interactions, author or mutate spells, and prepare a loadout.
5. Push into the next district with stronger enemies and stranger anti-witch defenses.

Design priority
The signature feature is the rest-time brewing system at camp. The camp is not just a menu stop. It is the heart of the game.

The player should be able to:
- test unstable reagent combinations
- discover emergent spell behavior
- tune spell logic or side effects
- decide how much risk, backlash, and weirdness to tolerate
- build a custom combat kit that feels authored by a dangerous witch

The camp should feel like a temporary occult laboratory:
- cauldron in the middle
- folding worktables and iron tools
- jars, bones, salts, herbs, wax, ash, moths, and organs on display
- dangling charms and notes pinned to the carriage wall
- a visible sense that the player is living on the road while being hunted

Common combat rules
- Combat is first-person and fast.
- Mid-combat brewing is real-time and stressful.
- Mid-combat brewing never uses both queued brew and coat rummage in the same variant.
- Every spell, even direct damage tools, must still feel like a curse or witch effect.
- The player can be slowed, exposed, or partially committed while brewing, but still needs enough control for a high-mobility FPS.

Common enemy ladder
- Torch mobs: farmers, zealots, panicked villagers
- Hardliners: musketeers, dog handlers, trappers, executioners
- Purifiers: censer bearers, salt grenadiers, reliquary snipers, hymn chanters
- Specialists: alchemical witch-breakers, oathbound hunter captains, ward engineers
- Constructs: iron saints, wicker penitents, bell-driven watchers, anti-curse frames

Shared reagent fantasy
Reagents should sound tactile, occult, and a little disgusting:
- grave salt
- saint ash
- black wax
- widow nettle
- bell bronze filings
- moth dust
- bog marrow
- plague honey
- funeral oil
- church glass splinters
- thorn hearts
- execution smoke
- raven tongues
- drowned pearl slurry

Variants in this folder
The four variant folders all describe the same game, but each changes:
- the spell-building mechanism at camp
- the style of emergent interactions
- the mid-combat brewing method
- the UI philosophy for combat and camp

Variant summary
1. Grimoire of Embers: structured symbolic spellcraft, queued brew
2. Satchel of Thorns: tactile hedge-witch alchemy, coat rummage
3. Cauldron of Echoes: layered ritual engineering, queued brew
4. Witchwork Engine: dense node-based witch programming, coat rummage
